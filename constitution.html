Constitution & Bylaws

Constitution of Dressage Club at Auburn

ARTICLE I – Name and Affiliations

Section I. Name
The name of this organization shall be the Dressage Club at Auburn.

Section II. Affiliations
The Club shall be associated with and subject to the rules of the Intercollegiate Dressage Association (IDA) and shall adhere to the guidelines of Auburn University and its Center for Student Organizations.

ARTICLE II – Purpose

Section I. Purpose
The purpose of the Club shall be to:

  1. Function as a collegiate dressage team and participate in IDA-sanctioned competitions.
  2. Promote and encourage dressage education, equestrian activities, and horsemanship.
  3. Foster safe riding practices, animal welfare, and teamwork.
  4. Provide opportunities for riders of all levels to train, compete, and develop leadership and sportsmanship.

ARTICLE III – Membership

Section I. Eligibility
Membership shall be open to regularly enrolled Auburn University students, faculty, and staff. Associate membership may be granted to non-enrolled individuals (such as alumni, spouses, or community members), but they may not vote or hold office.

Section II. Non-Discrimination
No student may be denied membership based on race, sex, religion, national origin, color, age, disability, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, or veteran status in accordance with Auburn University policy.

Section III. Voting Members
Voting members are those in good standing who have:

  • Paid dues for the semester.
  • Been placed on the official roster.
  • Attended at least ½ of regular meetings in the semester.

ARTICLE IV – Officers

Section I. Officers
The elected officers of the Club shall include:

  • President
  • Vice President
  • Secretary
  • Treasurer
  • Competition Captain (IDA Liaison)
  • Fundraising Chair
  • Communications Chair
  • Social Chair

Section II. Term of Office
Officers shall serve one-year terms beginning June 1 each year. Officers must be members in good standing.

Section III. Vacancies
If an office becomes vacant, the remaining officers may appoint a replacement until the next election.

Section IV. Removal
An officer failing to perform duties may be removed by unanimous consent of the other officers or a two-thirds vote of the membership.

ARTICLE V – Advisor

The Club shall have a faculty or staff advisor at all times. Advisor duties include:

  • Providing continuity and guidance.
  • Assisting with compliance regarding university policies (risk management, finances, etc.).
  • Being listed as a signatory on the Club account.
  • Attending officer/organization meetings when possible.

An advisor may be removed by a majority vote of officers if duties are not fulfilled.

ARTICLE VI – Meetings

  • The Club shall hold at least one meeting per month.
  • Special meetings may be called by any officer with two days' notice.

ARTICLE VII – Amendments

This constitution may be amended by a three-fourths vote of members in good standing.

Bylaws of Auburn University Dressage Club

ARTICLE I – Membership

Section I. Dues
Membership requires payment of dues each semester. Failure to pay dues within two months of notice results in removal from the roster.

Section II. Types of Membership

1. Competing Member

  • Pays $150 per semester.
  • Eligible to compete on IDA teams.
  • Priority for lessons, clinics, and funding (subject to budget).
  • May receive support for travel/entry fees to IDA competitions when funds allow.
  • Lesson 1x a week and participate in ride-a-test lessons before competitions.

2. Non-Competing Member

  • Pays $50 per semester.
  • May attend meetings, socials, and clinics (without club funding) or as auditors.
  • May wear team apparel and assist at shows and fundraisers.
  • Encouraged to lesson but not required.

Section III. Points System
A points system will track member involvement (attendance, volunteering, fundraising, etc.). Expectations will be set by the President at the start of each semester. Points may be used to determine eligibility for travel or priority in lessons/clinics.

ARTICLE II – Officer Duties

  • President: Oversees all club functions, presides over meetings, serves as main contact for IDA and university.
  • Vice President: Assists President, manages scheduling, supports officer activities.
  • Secretary: Maintains roster, keeps meeting minutes, manages attendance and club records.
  • Treasurer: Manages finances, collects dues, oversees accounts.
  • Competition Captain (IDA Liaison): Handles IDA team entries, coordinates travel, ensures eligibility compliance.
  • Fundraising Chair: Organizes fundraisers each semester.
  • Communications Chair: Manages social media, announcements, and website (if applicable).
  • Social Chair: Organizes bonding and service events to promote team spirit.

ARTICLE III – Elections

  • Elections held at the final spring semester meeting.
  • Candidates must be dues-paid members in good standing.
  • Officers elected by majority vote.
  • Vacancies may be filled by special election or officer appointment.

ARTICLE IV – Finances

  • The Treasurer manages funds with oversight from President and Advisor.
  • Two officer signatures (President/Treasurer) plus Advisor required for major withdrawals.

ARTICLE V – Parliamentary Authority

The Club shall follow Robert's Rules of Order unless inconsistent with this Constitution and Bylaws.

ARTICLE VI – Amendments

Bylaws may be amended by a two-thirds vote of members in good standing.
