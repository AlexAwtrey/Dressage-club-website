Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need riding experience to join?

No! We welcome riders of all levels, from complete beginners to experienced competitors. Our coach works with each member to develop their skills at their own pace.

Do I need my own horse?

No, you do not need your own horse to participate. We work with local stables that provide horses for our lessons and shows.

What is dressage?

Dressage is often described as "horse ballet." It's a highly skilled form of riding where horse and rider perform precise movements from memory. Dressage develops harmony, balance, and communication between horse and rider.

How much does it cost to join?

Competing members: $150 per semester - This covers club membership and IDA registration.

Non-competing members: $50 per semester - For those who want to participate in practices and club activities without competing in shows.

Additional costs: Lessons are separate and paid directly to the coach. Club funding through the university will help offset travel, show entry fees, facility rentals, equipment, and more!

When and where do you practice?

We practice at Simplicity Horsemanship. Check our Schedule page for the most current information, or visit our Socials page to book a lesson with Coach Kelley.

How do I compete with the IDA?

As a club member, you're eligible to compete at IDA shows. We'll help you register and prepare for your first show. Shows are held throughout the academic year at various locations.
